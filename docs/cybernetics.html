cybernetics

what is cybernetics?

short definition

kybernetes (ancient greek): steersman
"the field concerned with the information flows between *all media*, including biological and mechanical, and perhaps even cosmological." -norbert wiener, 1948
a cybernetic system is self-regulating, encompassing both animal and machine (through simplification)

theory of communication

claude shannon (1948)
information is a function of message probabilities, detached from context

evolution of cybernetics

1st phase: disembodied information, separate from context
2nd phase: including the observer as part of the system
3rd phase: overlaying multiple systems on top of each other (virtual objects, spimes)

the computer as media

the social, cultural, and theoretical impact of cybernetics has been associated with its tendency to reconfigure boundaries, and include more and more things as media
the computer enabled proof of cybernetic theory, and therefore became a media of choice

contribution to media studies

the idea of the feedback loop joined with a quantitative definition of information
the idea of a theoretical framework capable of analyzing control and communication in animals and machines
objects being able to instantiate these ideas

science and technology studies

So cybernetics is a good example of the how technological development drives our understanding of the world and ourselves: because computers can do so much, we tend to make comparisons between their operations and the natural world.
The material and technological levels of computational media correspond to cybernetic artifacts, while the semiotic level is routinely understood as information flows, and the social contexts involve myriad feedback loops between humans and computers that continue to reconfigure social, economic, and technological conditions for people throughout the world.
